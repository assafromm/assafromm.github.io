About

I am a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in the Jerusalem School of Business Administration at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, and a member of the Federmann Center for the Study of Rationality.

My research focuses on market design and matching theory. I am particularly interested in the design of matching markets—centralized procedures that assign people to positions, schools, or programs—and in understanding how behavioral considerations shape the performance of these mechanisms in practice.

In recent years I have been involved in redesigning several real-world Israeli matching markets, including the Israeli medical internship match, the Israeli psychology master's match, and the Israeli gap-year programs match.
